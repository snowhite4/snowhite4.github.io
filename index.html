IT Project

My project is based on a real time life monitor for use in combat scenarios and military deployment worldwide and send back real time data on vital signs such as heartrate, blood pressure and oxygen stats. The vitals signs would be taken by use of monitoring equipment such as heart rate monitors and oximeters and provide a benchmark on which to triage soldiers in trouble into different categories depending on the severity of their condition, the device would small enough so as not to interfere with the soldier’s duty, the device could be used to determine whether or not the soldier was in difficulty such as suffocating or bleeding out.

\The main hardware for this project would be wearable technology similar to an arm band or a Fitbit that would display to a commander or monitor at base camp how the soldier is travelling allowing people at the monitoring centre or field base to dispatch appropriate care such as medevac units or more combat units. The device would have ballistic elements such as being explosion proof and tamper proof to stop enemies from using the technology against the utiliser. It would also include an emergency button to enable the user to send an instant alert to base requesting assistance. The device would also have protection against dust and other protections that would allow it to function effectively on the battlefield, this is an important aspect of the device as dust could potentially cause the device to malfunction and assign vital military resources to a situation where there is nothing wrong. The technology would add to the commander’s ability to conduct warfare and reduce his death ratios and also improve the ability to respond to emergencies with an appropriate solution. It would also provide a notification service to notify the commander’s/monitors about a potentially life-threatening situation. The device would also be used to understand a unit/platoon’s ability to operate in any environment and provide the statistical analysis required for better battlefield management.

The main concept behind the device would be after a traumatic event to the user, the device would then send an encrypted communication via satellite or internet to the commander or life-signs monitor who would then from the available monitoring options (tablet, laptop) receive the transmission and evaluate it, they would then be able to assign a rescue team or medevac unit to rush to the scene to help the user, another implementation is the monitoring device would register the alerts and triage them into categories so that the person monitoring could evaluate which user’s to help first. I believe the device would be beneficial in certain situations better than other’s such as if the user is attacked suddenly by a hidden element and it is not seen by his immediate platoon. It would also be useful to pilots if they downed and forced to spend the night in the wilderness.
Advantages:

• Better response to emergency situations.


• A more in depth understanding of a unit/platoon’s physiology in real time.


• Real time updates on health and vital signs.


• Better statistical analysis of the battlefield


Disadvantages:

• Potential to fall into enemy hands and trigger false emergency situations.


• Potentially provide misleading data


A good example for the use of the device would be the 2002 battle of Takur Ghar, in which American Navy Seals were ambushed by Al Qaeda, the command didn’t know the situation on the ground and sent 3 chinook helicopters that were subsequently shot down.
